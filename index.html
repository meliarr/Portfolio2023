Susan Ruiz for Kansas

Visit Site

Running for a second term in 2020, Susan needed a website that would help voters, donors, and volunteers get to know her.

With a thoughtful approach for SEO, Accessibility, and UX, I put together a design that reflected her progressive values and celebrated the state of Kansas and the people of her district.

Through her website, she was able to increase her digital reach to receive campaign donations, volunteer signups, and share her story. Susan won her re-election in 2020 and in 2022.

Reframe Financial Planning

Visit Site

Inspired to help people live a meaningful retirement, Mary wanted a website that was inviting and reflective of her approach to financial planning: accessible, values-focused, and jargon-free.

I used imagery that reflected her target audience living a fulfilling retirement and organized the information in a way that would let visitors get to know her and her approach.

This was built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript on the Twenty Over Ten (now FMG) CMS platform for financial advisors.

Chris Herron for Lenexa

Visit Site

Inspired to run for the Lenexa City Council, Chris wanted a website that he and his campaign volunteers could easily update and manage to post events, press releases, and new content as his campaign prepares for the primary election on August 1, 2023.

Using Adobe Illustrator, I created a design that visually aligned with his branding, highlighted his passion for Lenexa and introduces his vision for the city.

The design was shared with a developer who built the site on WordPress using Elementor.
